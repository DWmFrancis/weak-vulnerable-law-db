Presidential Pardon Power

Citation

U.S. Constitution, Article II, Section 2

Risk Score

4 (High)

Vulnerability Types

  • Moral Hazard
  • Norm Dependency

Summary

Grants the President power to pardon federal crimes without oversight or constraints.

Historical Usage

Used controversially by several presidents, including pardons of political allies.

Flagged Abuse Scenarios

Presidents pardoning co-conspirators or incentivizing silence during investigations.

Suggested Reforms

Require public justification for pardons; limit scope for self-pardons or obstructive use.
