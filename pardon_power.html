Pardon Power – Article II, Section 2

Watchlist
Risk Score:
3.8 / 5
Vulnerability Types:
  • Absolute Discretion
  • Norm Dependency
  • Moral Hazard
Summary:

This clause grants the President the power to issue pardons for federal crimes. It has traditionally been used to offer mercy or correct injustices, but in recent decades, it has also been wielded as a political tool — including potential self-pardons, pre-emptive pardons, or pardons of allies to obstruct investigations.

Historical Use:
  • Nixon was pardoned by Ford (1974)
  • Controversial Trump pardons of political allies (2020)
Flagged Scenarios:
  • Preemptive pardons to block testimony
  • Pardons in exchange for loyalty or silence
  • Attempted self-pardon to avoid prosecution
Suggested Reforms:
  • Explicit ban on self-pardons
  • Require disclosure of pardon rationale
  • Trigger judicial review in corruption-related cases
