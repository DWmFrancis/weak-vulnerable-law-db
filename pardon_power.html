Pardon Power

Citation: U.S. Constitution, Article II, Section 2

Risk Score: 4 (Significant)

Vulnerability Types: Lack of Accountability, Moral Hazard

Summary: Grants the President the power to issue pardons and reprieves for federal offenses, excluding impeachment.

Historical Usage: Nixon was pardoned by Ford; recent controversial pardons include allies and political supporters.

Flagged Abuse Scenarios: Self-pardons or pardons used to obstruct investigations or reward political loyalty.

Suggested Reforms: Prohibit self-pardons; require DOJ review and public justification for controversial pardons.
