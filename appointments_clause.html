Appointments Clause

Citation: U.S. Constitution, Article II, Section 2, Clause 2

Risk Score: 4 (Significant)

Vulnerability Types: Executive Overreach, Loophole Exploitation

Summary: Governs how public officials are appointed, allowing the President to appoint with Senate confirmation or via recess appointments.

Historical Usage: Recess appointments have been used to bypass Senate resistance; challenged in court (e.g., NLRB v. Noel Canning).

Flagged Abuse Scenarios: Use of acting appointments to bypass Senate scrutiny; politicized hiring of key agency heads.

Suggested Reforms: Limit duration of acting roles; require Senate confirmation for key executive positions.
