Appointments Clause – Article II, Section 2

High Risk
Risk Score:
4.3 / 5
Vulnerability Types:
  • Ambiguity
  • Oversight Weakness
  • Norm Dependency
  • Precedent Loopholes
Summary:

This constitutional clause grants the President authority to appoint federal officers with Senate confirmation. It also allows for temporary appointments during Senate recesses. In recent years, this clause has been stretched to install acting officials without proper confirmation or prolonged "acting" leadership to bypass Senate oversight.

Historical Use:
  • Use of "acting" roles to avoid Senate confirmation
  • Recess appointments made during pro forma Senate sessions
Flagged Scenarios:
  • Appointment of loyalists as "acting" heads indefinitely
  • Weaponizing non-confirmed leadership to bypass norms
Suggested Reforms:
  • Limit duration of acting appointments
  • Clarify pro forma session rules in legislation
  • Mandate Senate review for key executive roles within 90 days
