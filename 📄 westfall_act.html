Defamation Law (Westfall Act)

Vulnerability:

Shielding officials from civil liability

Overview:

The Westfall Act allows the Department of Justice to declare an official’s actions part of their duties—substituting the U.S. as defendant in civil lawsuits.

Exploited by:

DOJ tried to block E. Jean Carroll’s lawsuit against Trump by invoking Westfall protections.

Key Weaknesses:

  • Grants broad protection from accountability
  • DOJ discretion unchecked
  • Can retroactively erase private liability

Reform Ideas:

  • Require independent review before substitution
  • Prohibit use in defamation or personal conduct cases
  • Create judicial override mechanism
