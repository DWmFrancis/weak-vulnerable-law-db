Insurrection Act – 10 U.S. Code §§ 251–255

Status: High Risk

This act allows the President to deploy military forces within the United States under vague and broad circumstances. It lacks adequate congressional oversight or judicial review, which makes it prone to misuse during political crises or civil unrest.

Known Cases or Incidents

  • Used during the 1992 Los Angeles riots.
  • Cited as a potential justification during the 2020 George Floyd protests.
  • Considered in the context of January 6, 2021.

Identified Vulnerabilities

  • Vague language allows broad interpretation.
  • Lack of judicial or congressional checks.
  • Can be invoked unilaterally by the President.

Potential Safeguards or Reforms

  • Require congressional authorization for domestic deployment.
  • Define precise conditions for use.
  • Introduce automatic judicial review within 72 hours.
