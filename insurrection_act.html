Insurrection Act – 10 U.S. Code §§ 251–255

Status: High Risk

Risk Score:

4.6 / 5

Vulnerability Types:

  • Ambiguity
  • Norm Dependency
  • Scope Creep
  • Moral Hazard

Summary:

This act allows the President to deploy military forces within the United States under vague and broad circumstances. It lacks adequate congressional oversight or judicial review, which makes it prone to misuse during political crises or civil unrest.

Historical Use:

  • Used during LA Riots (1992)
  • Considered during BLM protests

Flagged Scenarios:

  • Suppression of lawful protests
  • Federal control over dissenting states

Suggested Reforms:

  • Define key terms
  • Mandate Congressional review
  • Enable judicial oversight
