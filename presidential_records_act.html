Presidential Records Act – 44 U.S. Code §§ 2201–2209

Status: Vulnerable

Risk Score:

4.1 / 5

Vulnerability Types:

  • Weak Enforcement
  • Delayed Accountability

Summary:

Designed to preserve presidential documents, this law lacks real-time oversight and fails to prevent concealment or destruction of records. There are minimal penalties for noncompliance.

Historical Use:

  • Controversies over Trump-era records

Flagged Scenarios:

  • Secret destruction of key documents
  • Delays in archive access

Suggested Reforms:

  • Add enforceable penalties
  • Create real-time review system
