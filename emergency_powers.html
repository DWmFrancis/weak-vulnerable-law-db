Emergency Powers

Citation: National Emergencies Act, 50 U.S. Code §§ 1601–1651

Risk Score: 5 (High)

Vulnerability Types: Executive Overreach, Indefinite Scope

Summary: Allows the President to declare a national emergency and unlock various powers with minimal oversight.

Historical Usage: Over 70 emergency declarations still in effect; used for border wall funding in 2019.

Flagged Abuse Scenarios: Perpetual emergencies used to circumvent normal legislative processes or suppress dissent.

Suggested Reforms: Require congressional renewal every 90 days; limit available powers per emergency type.
