Emergency Powers – National Emergencies Act (50 U.S. Code § 1601 et seq.)

Status: Critical

Risk Score:

4.8 / 5

Vulnerability Types:

  • Unilateral Activation
  • Indefinite Duration
  • Insufficient Oversight

Summary:

Grants the President sweeping powers with little Congressional restraint. Emergencies can be declared easily and sustained indefinitely unless revoked by Congress.

Historical Use:

  • Trump's border wall declaration

Flagged Scenarios:

  • Perpetual state of emergency

Suggested Reforms:

  • Automatic sunset clauses
  • Require bipartisan renewal
